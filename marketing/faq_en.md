# Bridgy FAQ (English)

## Q1. What can this app do?
**A1.** Bridgy supports face-to-face communication using a two-board (top/bottom) layout. It displays text and translations to help with written conversation.

## Q2. How can I input text?
**A2.** You can use:
- Drawing/handwriting (finger / Apple Pencil)
- Keyboard input
- Scribble with Apple Pencil
- Voice input (speech-to-text)

## Q3. The microphone doesn’t work.
**A3.** On your iPad, check microphone permission:
**Settings → Privacy & Security → Microphone**, and allow access for this app.

## Q4. I can’t use translation. Please explain.
**A4.** The first time only, language data may need to be downloaded/prepared. Please connect to the internet and try again.

**Supported languages:** English, Spanish, French, Japanese, Vietnamese  
**Translation pairs:** 25 combinations (e.g., EN↔EN, EN↔ES, EN↔FR, EN↔JA, EN↔VI, etc.)

### 1) Translation mode settings
**Manual translation mode** (you confirm that the input was correctly converted to text before translating)  
- Tap the **→** button to the right of the microphone.  
- The translation will be shown in your conversation partner’s language.

**Auto translation mode** (you do not confirm the text each time)  
- After speech input ends, the app automatically translates into your partner’s language.

### Language setup
- Tap the **gear (Settings)** icon and set **Your language** and **Partner’s language**.
- Select:
  - The language you speak
  - The language your partner speaks

### Choosing who is speaking (for speech input)
- When **you** speak: tap **You** (next to the microphone).  
  → Your speech is translated and shown in your partner’s language.
- When **your partner** speaks: tap **Partner** (next to the microphone).  
  → Your partner’s speech is translated and shown in your language.

### 2) Speech input (voice-to-text)
- Tap the **microphone** icon (the mic color changes).
- Tap the **microphone** icon again to stop recording.  
  → What you said will appear as text on the screen.

### 3) Keyboard input
- Tap the bottom menu: **Enter text (Pen/Keyboard)**  
  → A new window opens.
- Choose **who is typing**: **You** or **Partner**
- Select **Your language** and **Partner’s language**
- Tap the white input area to show the keyboard
- Type text and tap **Add**  
  → The text will be displayed in the selected language (and translated according to the translation mode).

**Important:** If you register common phrases in the iPad **Text Replacement / User Dictionary**, you can input long sentences with short shortcuts. This is very convenient.  
Example: “Nice to meet you. I’m ___.”  
Set it here: **Settings → General → Keyboard → Text Replacement**.

### 4) Apple Pencil (Scribble) input
- On iPad, check:
  **Settings → Apple Pencil**
  - “Only Draw with Apple Pencil”: **OFF**
  - “Scribble”: **ON**
- Tap the bottom menu: **Enter text (Pen/Keyboard)**  
  → A new window opens.
- Choose **who is writing**: **You** or **Partner**
- Select **Your language** and **Partner’s language**
- Write in the white area with Apple Pencil, then tap **Add**  
  → Text will appear on the screen and translate according to the translation mode.

## Q5. What is the difference between Auto and Manual translation?
**A5.**  
- **Manual:** Tap the **→** button (next to the microphone) to translate.
- **Auto:** The app translates automatically after you stop recording.

## Q6. Auto translation doesn’t work only on the first try.
**A6.** The first time only, language data may need to be downloaded/prepared. Please use **Manual translation** for the first translation. From the second time onward, Auto translation should work.

## Q7. What is the difference between the Eraser and the Trash can?
**A7.**
- **Eraser:** Removes drawn lines.
- **Trash can:** Clears **everything** (lines and text).

## Q8. Can I zoom in/out and move text?
**A8.**
- Use **two-finger pinch** to zoom in/out.
- To move text, drag it with **one finger**.  
  **Tip:** Tap the **Eraser** first to reduce accidental drawing while moving items.

## Q9. Can I change the default text size?
**A9.** Yes. Touch the bottom slider bar:
- When it shows **“Text”**, move the slider left/right to change text size.
- If you do not switch to “Text”, the slider adjusts line thickness instead.

## Q10. Do I have to agree to the Terms to use the app?
**A10.** Yes. If you do not agree, the app will not function.

## Q11. Can I use it offline?
**A11.** Yes. However, **only the first time you use translation**, you may need to download language data, which requires an internet connection.

## Q12. When I pinch to zoom or move text, a line is drawn by mistake. Why?
**A12.** Because the app supports drawing with your finger, the first touch can be recognized as “drawing,” which may create an unintended line.  
Before zooming or moving text, tap the **Eraser** icon first. This makes it less likely to draw lines accidentally, and zoom/move becomes smoother.

## Q13. Lines are hard to see.
**A13.** Depending on iPad display/accessibility settings (such as **Invert Colors** or **High Contrast**), lines may be hard to see.  
Try turning those settings OFF:

- **Settings → Accessibility → Display & Text Size**
  - (Invert Colors / Increase Contrast, etc.)
