Bridgy FAQ (English)

Frequently asked questions and basic usage notes for Bridgy.

Q1. What can this app do?

A1. Bridgy is an app that helps “bridge” face-to-face communication through on-screen text display, writing/drawing, and translation.

Q2. What input methods are available?

A2. You can use the following input methods.
  • Line drawing (finger / Apple Pencil)
  • Voice input (speech to text)
  • Keyboard input
  • Apple Pencil Scribble (handwriting input)

Q3. The microphone does not work.

A3. Please check microphone permission in iPad Settings → Privacy & Security → Microphone.

Q4. Translation does not work. Please explain in detail.

A4. The first time only, language data must be downloaded and prepared. Please connect to the internet and try again.

Supported languages: English / Spanish / French / Japanese / Vietnamese / Italian

Translation pairs: 36 pairs (for example: English↔English, English↔Spanish, English↔French, English↔Japanese, English↔Vietnamese, English↔Italian)

1) Translation mode settings

Manual translation (confirm that the recognized text is correct before translating)

  • Tap the arrow mark → to the right of the microphone
  • The translation will be shown in the other person’s language

Automatic translation after recording stops (translates without checking whether the recognized text is correct)

  • After recording stops, the text is automatically translated into the other person’s language

Language settings

  • Tap the gear icon (Settings) and set the language for “Self” and “Partner”
  • Language selection
    • Select your spoken language
    • Select the other person’s spoken language

Who is speaking? (voice input)

  • When you speak: Tap Self next to the microphone → your speech is translated into the other person’s language and shown on their side
  • When the other person speaks: Tap Partner next to the microphone → their speech is translated into your language and shown on your side

2) Voice input

  • Tap the microphone icon (the microphone color changes)
  • Tap the microphone icon again to stop → the spoken words are converted to text, shown on screen, and translated

3) Keyboard input

  • Tap “Enter Text (Pen / Keyboard)” in the bottom menu → a new window opens
  • Select who is entering the text: Self / Partner
  • Select your language / the partner’s language
  • Tap the white area → the keyboard appears
  • Enter text from the keyboard and tap Add → text appears on screen in the selected language (and is translated according to the translation mode)
Important: It is convenient to register fixed phrases in User Dictionary (Text Replacement). A short trigger word can display a long sentence at once.
Example: “Nice to meet you. My name is ○○. Thank you.”
Setting path: Settings → General → Keyboard → Text Replacement

4) Apple Pencil input (Scribble) — this feature does not work on iPhone

  • iPad settings: Settings → Apple Pencil
    • Only Draw with Apple Pencil: OFF
    • Scribble: ON
  • Tap “Enter Text (Pen / Keyboard)” in the bottom menu → a new window opens
  • Select who is entering the text: Self / Partner
  • Select your language / the partner’s language
  • Write in the white area with Apple Pencil → tap Add → the text appears on screen and is translated according to the translation mode

5) Voice output

  • Tap the speaker icon in the bottom menu to have the translated text read aloud

Q6. Automatic translation does not work the first time only.

A6. The first time only, language data must be downloaded and prepared. For the very first translation, please use the manual translate button. Automatic translation will work from the second time onward.

Q7. What is the difference between the eraser and the trash can?

A7.
  • Eraser: Erases drawn lines
  • Trash can: Clears both lines and text

Q8. Can I enlarge, reduce, or move displayed text?

A8.
  • You can zoom in and out with a two-finger pinch
  • You can move the text position by dragging with one finger
    To reduce accidental drawing while moving text, tap the eraser first

Q9. Can I change the text size and line thickness?

A9. Yes. You can change the text size by moving the slider at the bottom of the screen left or right. Tapping the slider switches it to line thickness adjustment.

Q10. Do I have to agree to the Terms of Use?

A10. Yes. If you do not agree, the app will not function.

Q11. Can I use it offline?

A11. Yes. However, the first time you use the translation feature, the required language data must be downloaded, so an online connection is necessary.

Q12. Pinch gestures and moving text do not work well, and lines are drawn by mistake. Why?

A12. Because this app allows line drawing with a finger, the moment your finger first touches the screen may be recognized as drawing, and a line may appear. When zooming or moving text, tap the eraser icon first, then perform the gesture. This makes accidental lines less likely and helps zooming and text movement work more smoothly.

Q13. The lines are hard to see.

A13. Depending on iPad display settings such as Smart Invert, Classic Invert, or increased contrast, lines may become hard to see. Please try turning those settings off.
Setting path: Settings → Accessibility → Display & Text Size → (Invert / Contrast)
